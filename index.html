About

I work inside operating teams to improve how work moves through an organization.

As companies grow, execution problems usually show up in a few predictable places: ownership becomes unclear, systems fragment, data becomes unreliable, and coordination between teams breaks down.

My role is to bring structure to those areas — designing the operational systems that support consistent execution.

Over the past fifteen years I’ve worked across education, financial services, and SaaS-adjacent businesses in roles including Chief of Staff, Sales Operations Director, and Operations Lead. My work typically involves revenue systems, CRM architecture, reporting infrastructure, and cross-functional execution.

I operate directly inside the work: mapping current processes, redesigning workflows, implementing systems, and supporting teams as those systems become part of daily operations.

Approach

A consistent sequence for operational work.

The work changes by company. The sequence usually does not.

Step 1

Map the current system

Understand how work actually moves through the organization, including tools, ownership, handoffs, and failure points.

Step 2

Define structure

Clarify ownership, standards, and expectations around how work should move across teams.

Step 3

Implement systems

Design workflows, CRM structure, reporting frameworks, automation logic, and supporting documentation.

Step 4

Make performance visible

Build dashboards and operating rhythms that allow leadership teams to monitor execution and identify issues early.

Step 5

Transfer ownership

Ensure the system remains functional without ongoing external support by training the right owners and tightening the operating model.

Step 6

Refine where needed

As growth creates new constraints, the system is adjusted without rebuilding everything from scratch.
